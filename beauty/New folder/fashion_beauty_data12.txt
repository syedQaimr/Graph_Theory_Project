Having chapped and dry lips in winter is a common problem most people can relate to. To tackle this issue, people often turn to various solutions, with one effective approach being the application of lip balms. However, did you realise that making your own lip balm allows you to tailor the ingredients to your preferences and target specific lip concerns? Natural ingredients provide healing qualities that can help relieve chapped and dry lips while also improving overall lip health.Beeswax: Protects moisture by acting as a natural emollient Coconut Oil: Adds hydration and has antibacterial properties, promoting healthy lips Shea Butter: Shea butter is rich in moisturising properties and helps soothe and heal dry lips Essential Oils: Provide fragrance and additional therapeutic benefits. Popular choices include lavender, peppermint, and chamomile Honey: Offers natural sweetness and has antibacterial properties that aid in healing Vitamin E Oil: Acts as an antioxidant, promoting skin health and aiding in the preservation of the lip balm  According to a review published in ResearchGate, natural lip balm can be created with naturally occurring bases, oils, extracts, colour, and flavouring agents. These ingredients can be assessed for their ability to withstand temperature changes, taste well, apply smoothly, adhere well, and be removed with ease, among other qualities. Also Read: Secret To Soft And Moisturised Lips: Here's Why You Should Apply Castor Oil On Your Lips DIY Healing Lip Balm Recipes  Lavender Lip Balm Ingredients  2 tablespoons beeswax pellets 2 tablespoons coconut oil 1 tablespoon shea butter 10 drops of lavender essential oil 1 teaspoon honey 1 capsule of vitamin E oil (optional)  Instructions  Take a double boiler and melt beeswax, coconut oil, and shea butter together until it fully liquefies. Remove from heat and add lavender essential oil, honey, and vitamin E oil. Stir it until it mixes well. Next, pour the mixture into lip balm tubes or small containers. Let it cool and solidify before use. Add peppermint essential oil, honey, and vitamin E oil. Mix thoroughly. Once the mixture has cooled, pour it into lip balm containers.     Chamomile Lip Balm Ingredients  2 tablespoons beeswax pellets 1.5 tablespoons coconut oil 2 tablespoons shea butter 10 drops of chamomile essential oil 1 teaspoon honey 1 capsule of vitamin E oil (optional)  Instructions  Using a double boiler, melt the coconut oil, beeswax, and shea butter together. Add chamomile essential oil, honey, and vitamin E oil. Mix it well Pour the mixture into lip balm tubes or containers and let it set.  Tips for Making the Perfect Lip Balm  Experiment with Ratios: Adjust the quantities of ingredients to achieve your preferred consistency and level of hydration. Use High-Quality Ingredients: Opt for organic, unrefined versions of oils and butter for maximum benefits. Be Mindful of Essential Oils: Essential oils add fragrance and therapeutic properties, but be cautious not to use too much, as they can be potent.
